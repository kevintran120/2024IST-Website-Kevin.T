What is the King's Gambit?

The King's Gambit is a chess opening that originated no later than the 16th century. It was one of the most popular openings for around 300 years, in which it has been played by most of the strongest players ever.

How do you play the King's Gambit?

The King's Gambit is a chess opening that is executed by moving white's pawns to e4 and f4 with black's pawn being placed at e5. When doing this, white offers a pawn to black to divert it from the center.

King's Gambit accepted

If black accepts the sacrifice from white and takes the pawn at f4, white's main continuations are the King's Knight gambit (3.Nf3) and the Bishop's Gambit (3.Bc4).
